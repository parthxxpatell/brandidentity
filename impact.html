The Human Cost

The smoke from crop burning contains a cocktail of harmful pollutants: PM2.5, carbon monoxide, methane, and volatile organic compounds. These don't just irritate; they kill.

  • Respiratory Illnesses: Asthma, bronchitis, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) are exacerbated.
  • Cardiovascular Disease: Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) can enter the bloodstream, increasing the risk of heart attacks and strokes.
  • Reduced Visibility: Dense smog leads to road accidents and disrupts daily life.
  • Mental Health: The constant exposure to pollution and its health consequences contribute to stress and anxiety.

The Environmental Cost

Beyond the immediate air quality crisis, crop burning inflicts lasting damage on the very land that sustains us.

  • Soil Degradation: Burning destroys beneficial microorganisms, reduces soil fertility, and depletes essential nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.
  • Greenhouse Gas Emissions: Releases significant amounts of carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide, contributing to climate change.
  • Loss of Biodiversity: Harms insects, birds, and other wildlife that rely on healthy soil ecosystems.
  • Water Pollution: Ash and pollutants can contaminate water sources, affecting aquatic life and human health.
