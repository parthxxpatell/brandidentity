When Fields Burn, Cities Choke

Every autumn, the air quality index in North India doesn't just rise—it suffocates. Farmers burn 23 million tonnes of stubble because there's no economic alternative. The smoke travels hundreds of kilometers, turning skies grey and lungs heavy.

But here's the truth we choose to see:
This isn't just waste. It's raw material waiting for purpose.
